Dr. Evelyn Boyd Granville, PhD

African-American Mathematician

Evelyn Boyd Granville was the second African-American woman to earn a doctorate in Mathematics.

The excellent education and training I was privileged to receive place upon me an obligation to improve educational opportunities for others.
Dr. Evelyn Boyd Granville, My Life as a Mathematician

Evelyn Boyd Granville was born on May 1, 1924, in Washington DC.

Growing up at a challenging time, Evelyn Boyd Granville went to Dunbar High School. Despite being segregated, the school was renowned for its academic excellence.

A gifted student, Evelyn Boyd Granville first pursued an education in Language and Humanities, and later on studied astronomy at Smith College. She decided to major in Mathematics and graduated summe cum laude in 1945

A gifted student, Evelyn Boyd Granville first pursued an education in Language and Humanities, and later on studied astronomy at Smith College. She decided to major in Mathematics and graduated summe cum laude in 1945

She pursued a doctorate at Yale University, and graduated in 1949 with a doctoral dissertation "On Laguerre series in the complex domain"

She pursued a fulfilling career as a researcher and as a teacher, at Fisk University, California State University, Los Angeles, and Texas College.

She also worked for the National Standards Bureau, IBM, NASA, and the North American Aviation Company.

A dedicated educator, she retired in 1997 (aged 73), seven years after being appointed to the Sam A. Lindsey Chair of the Mathematics Department at the University of Texas.

Achievements and Awards

  • Master in Mathematics, 1946, Smith College
  • PhD in Mathematics, 1949, Yale University
  • Julius Rosenwald Fellowship, Yale
  • Atomic Energy Commission Predoctoral Fellowship, Yale
  • Honorary doctorate, 1989, Smith College
